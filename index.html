About Me

I am a first-year Master's student at Beijing Technology and Business University (BTBU), supervised by Prof. Xiaochuan Wang. My research focuses on Point Cloud Quality Assessment (PCQA), where I develop robust methods for evaluating and improving the quality of 3D point cloud data.

My current work involves exploring novel deep learning architectures for point cloud processing and quality evaluation, with particular emphasis on developing metrics that align with human perception of visual quality in 3D environments.

Research Interests

Point Cloud Quality Assessment

Development of objective and subjective metrics for evaluating the perceptual quality of 3D point clouds, including methods that correlate with human visual perception.

3D Vision & Processing

Advanced techniques for processing and analyzing 3D point cloud data, including registration, segmentation, and classification for various computer vision applications.

Deep Learning for Point Clouds

Application of deep neural networks to point cloud data, developing architectures specifically designed to handle the unordered and irregular nature of point cloud structures.

Current Research

My current research focuses on developing a novel framework for point cloud quality assessment that combines geometric features with perceptual attributes. The proposed method aims to overcome limitations in existing metrics by incorporating both objective measurements and subjective human perception factors.

This approach has potential applications in various domains including virtual reality, autonomous driving, and digital twins where high-quality 3D representations are critical.
